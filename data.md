**Listen to the following dialogue between a husband and his wife on the importance of family in Islam. Pay special attention to the new vocabulary.** 

**Husband** : What were you watching dear? 

4 

**Wife** : I was watching a program about family in the light of Islam. 

**Husband** : What was it discussing? 

**Wife** : The guest was speaking about how Allah made man His Vicegerent of earth and entrusted him with the population of earth. 

**Husband** : This is interesting. How can be man Allah’s vicegerent on earth? 

**Wife** : This can be attained through reproduction in an organized manner. 

**Husband** : Oh! You mean through marriage. Indeed Islam gives considerable importance to marriage as the basic step in forming the family which is the nucleus of society. 

**Wife** : Yes, family is the cornerstone in any society whether primitive or advanced. 

**Husband** : How did they describe the family in the program? 

**Wife** : They said that families provide their members with a sense of belonging, interdependence, and of being wanted. 

**Husband** : I believe that I heard the announcer say that there are three major relationships within a family? 

**Wife** : Yes indeed he said. The conjugal relation is that between husband and wife, the parent-child relationship and the sibling relationship; i.e. that between brothers, sisters, or between brothers and sisters. 

**Husband** : What did they say about the conjugal relationship? 

**Wife** : Islam denotes that the couple – husband and wife- should treat each other with mutual respect, love, and constant attention. This means that engagement in a job or occupation should not lead to inattentiveness or indifference by one towards the other. Spouses are equally responsible for looking after their children and bringing them in a sound healthy way. 

**Husband** : Does this mean that they have identical roles. 

**Wife** : No not identical. They have equally important roles in the family, but not identical roles. Their roles are complementary. In fact, the husband and wife in a Muslim family are given those responsibilities that best suit his or her nature. 

**Husband** : Can you explain further? 

5 

**Wife** : The wife’s role is to bring up the children and provide love and affection for her family members, while the husband is the breadwinner of the family who also provides protection for them. These roles complement each other. 

**Husband** : Yes, these roles should be based on mutual understanding and consultation. 

**Wife** : Indeed they should. While the mother is considered a tremendous source of strength to her husband in facing the hardships of life, the husband should always be the firm shield that protects the wife from the hazards of life. 

**Husband** : Did the program discuss the other relationships within the family. 

**Wife** : The announcer said that these will be discussed in the coming episodes. 

**Husband** : We should watch them together.

**Listen to the following dialogue between a TV announcer and his guest (Dr. Muhammad a reputable sociologist) on marriage as an Islamic institution. Pay special attention to the new vocabulary** 

**Husband** : When will the program about family relationships start? 

**Wife** : It will start in five minutes. 

**Husband** : (After five minutes) The program has just started dear. 

**Announcer** : Good afternoon ladies and gentlemen. Today we will discuss with our guest the importance of marriage in Islam. As we know marriage aims at establishing the family which is the first unit of society. Dr. Muhammad, would you please explain how can a man start a family? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : When a man plans to marry a certain woman, he makes a marriage proposal to her, to her parents, or her guardian. And it should be noted that no man else can propose to the same woman until the first man withdraws his proposal or is rejected. This is instructed in Islam to avoid enmity caused by quarreling over marrying a certain woman. 

**Announcer** : What happens if the woman accepts the marriage proposal? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : The man gives her a dowry; i.e. free gift. The amount of the downy in Islam depends on the financial ability of the husband and the social position of the wife. In many cases, the dowry is divided into two parts: an advanced dowry and a deferred dowry. The advance dowry is paid upon contracting marriage and the deferred dowry is paid upon the termination of the marriage whether by death or divorce. 

**Announcer** : Can a father or guardian force a woman to marry a certain man? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : As men have the right to choose their wives, women also have the right to choose their husbands. Of course, a young girl might not have enough experience to choose the right man. Therefore, the advice of the father or guardian 

12 

has its justification. But no father or guardian has the right to force a girl to get married to a man whom she doesn't accept. In other words, in Islam the consent of the woman to marry a certain man is essential. 

**Announcer** : We see nowadays many cases of divorce among young couples. So what should husband and wife do when there is misunderstanding between them? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : It is due to the teachings of Prophet Muhammad (PBUH) that divorce is the most hateful of permitted practices. Therefore, divorce takes place only rarely among Muslims compared with the large number of divorces in nonMuslim societies. A true Muslim faces the difficulties of married life and avoids the disruption of family relations as long as possible, turning to divorce only as a last resort. 

**Announcer** : What happens if the problems persist between them? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : All means of reconciliation must be done first. When either the husband or wife finds that it is impossible to continue living together, divorce must follow. 

**Announcer** : And can the woman, in this case, get married again. 

**Dr. Muhammad:** Yes, she can. But she must wait for a certain period before she gets married to another man. Also, a divorced pregnant woman cannot get married before giving birth. One reason for these regulations is to avoid any confusion that may arise concerning the father of the embryo. 

**Announcer:** I am afraid that the time of the program is over. Thank you Dr. Muhammad for the valuable information. And waiting for you in the coming episode to discuss the relationship between parents and their children in the light of Islam.

**Announcer** : Good afternoon ladies and gentlemen. Today we will resume our discussion with Dr. Muhammad about family relationships. And today we will shed light on parents’ responsibilities towards their children. Dr. Muhammad, could you explain the basic Islamic foundations that guarantee parental care for children? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : First, Islam advocates the proper naming of the children at birth, and then their proper upbringing and education until they reach maturity so that children be able to distinguish right from wrong according to the dictates of Islam 

**Announcer** : What are the responsibilities of the father and the mother in bringing up their children? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : The father is responsible for the financial support of his children. Charges for the child’s education are paid by the father. **Announcer** : Following the example of the Prophet (PBUH), children should be treated with gentleness and understanding, but what kind of punishment is allowed in Islam if the child makes some mistake? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : A moderate policy of punishment and reward should be applied by the parents in rearing their children; neither to be too harsh

**Announcer** : Can the parents give privilege to one child over the others? 

**Dr. Muhammad** : Parents should give equal attention to their children. They should not distinguish one over the others.

**Listen to the following dialogue between Ahmad and Mona on Inheritance in Islam. Pay special attention to the new vocabulary** 

**Ahmad:** Why are you wearing black Mona? 

**Mona:** I am going to the funeral of my friend Rasha’s father? 

**Ahmad:** May he rest in peace. He was a rich and very generous man. 

**Mona:** Certainly, his son will inherit all the estate since he is the only son, and I think that his daughters will not inherit anything. 

32 

**Ahmad:** Not in Islam. Before Islam, women were deprived of the right to inherit. When Islam came, it put an end to this injustice. 

**Mona:** Oh! Great! This means that his son and daughters will have a share in his property. 

**Ahmad:** Yes, but before distributing the property to heirs, two things should be done. First, the debts of the deceased person should be paid. Secondly, his will should be carried out. 

**Mona:** Oh! He could have written all his property to his son or to one of his daughters, or even to some charitable organization. 

**Ahmad:** No, indeed. In Islam, there are two conditions for will-making. First, the beneficiary of the will cannot be an heir, because an heir inherits without a will. In addition, making a will to an heir usually creates a grudge between this heir and other heirs. The second condition is that a person cannot go beyond one-third of his property in his will. The reason is that: it is unfair to deprive close relatives of inheritance by giving some individual or institution most of the property. 

**Mona:** This is fair. But who are the heirs? 

**Ahmad:** Heirs include the wife, the husband, all sons, all daughters, and both parents. They also include grandsons if the dead person’s sons are dead, the grandfather if his father is dead, and the grandmother if the mother is dead. _active_ “My aunt made this rug”

_passive_ This old rug **was made** by my aunt, but that rug **was made** by my sister.

- _direct_ ‘ **Don’t work** too hard,’ I said to Joe. 

- _reported_ I **told Joe not to work** too hard. 

- _direct_ ‘Can you help me, please,’ Jackie said to me. 

reported       Jackie **asked me to help** her. 

You can also say ‘Somebody **said (not) to** do something’: 

- -Paul **saidnot to worry** about him. ( _butnot_ Paul said me)

## **C-He asked me where …** 

Notice the changes in questions’ word order in reported speech. Compare: 

_-direct_ The police officer said to us ‘Where **are you going** ?’ 

_reported_ The police officer asked us where **we were going** . 

- _direct_ Clare asked ‘What time do **the shops close** ?’ 

_Reported_ Clare wanted to know what time **the shops closed** . 

In reported speech the verb usually changes to the past ( **were, closed** etc.). 

Study these examples. You had a job interview and the interviewer asked you these questions: 

1- **Are you willing** to travel?   2- **What do you do** in your spare time? 

3-How long **have you been working** in your present job? 4- **Why did you apply** for the job? 5- **Can you speak** any other languages? 

6- **Do you have** a driving license? 

Later you tell a friend what the interviewer asked you. You use reported speech: 

1-She asked if ( _or_ whether) **I** _**was**_ willing to travel. 2-She wanted to know what **I did** in my spare time. 3-She asked how long **I had been working** in my present job. 4-She asked why I had applied for the job. or … why I **applied** … 

5-She wanted to know if (or whether) I **could** speak any other languages. 

6-She asked if (or whether) I **had** a driving license.
